About Sum 41

Sum 41 is a Canadian rock band formed in 1996 in Ajax, Ontario. Known for their high-energy performances and iconic punk rock sound, the band has sold over 10 million albums worldwide. Their breakthrough album, All Killer No Filler, featured the hit single "Fat Lip," which topped the Billboard charts.

The current lineup includes Deryck Whibley (vocals and guitar), Dave Baksh (guitar), Jason McCaslin (bass), Tom Thacker (guitar and keyboards), and Frank Zummo (drums). With eight studio albums and numerous awards, Sum 41 continues to be a dominant force in the rock scene.

Band Members

Deryck Whibley

Role: Lead Vocals & Guitar

Deryck Whibley is the frontman and primary songwriter of Sum 41. He has been the driving force behind the band's success since its inception.

Dave Baksh

Role: Lead Guitar & Backing Vocals

Known as "Brownsound," Dave left the band in 2006 but returned in 2015, bringing back his signature guitar sound.

Jason McCaslin

Role: Bass & Backing Vocals

Nicknamed "Cone," Jason has been a core part of the band's rhythm section since 1999.

Tom Thacker

Role: Guitar & Keyboards

Tom joined Sum 41 in 2007 and adds depth to the band's sound with his multi-instrumental skills.

Frank Zummo

Role: Drums

Frank took over drumming duties in 2015 and is known for his energetic and precise drumming style.

Discography

Explore the complete collection of Sum 41's albums, from their debut to their latest releases.

All Killer No Filler (2001)

Featuring the hit singles "Fat Lip" and "In Too Deep," this album launched Sum 41 into mainstream success.

Does This Look Infected? (2002)

Includes fan-favorite tracks like "Still Waiting" and "The Hell Song." A more aggressive sound compared to their debut.

Chuck (2004)

Named after a UN peacekeeper, this album features "We're All to Blame" and "Pieces," showcasing a heavier sound.

Underclass Hero (2007)

A return to their punk roots, including tracks like "Walking Disaster" and "With Me."

Screaming Bloody Murder (2011)

A darker and more experimental album featuring "Blood in My Eyes."

13 Voices (2016)

Written after Deryck Whibley's recovery from alcoholism, this album includes "Fake My Own Death."

Order in Decline (2019)

One of the band's heaviest albums, featuring "Out for Blood."
